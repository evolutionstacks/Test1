Winnipeg's Top Roofing Services

Winnipeg’s fluctuating temperatures often leads to common roofing issues that homeowners should be aware of . These issues include moisture build-up leading to rot and mold .

  • Leaks and Ice Dams: Ice dams are a common issue in Winnipeg, particularly after heavy snowfalls.
  • Shingle Damage: Broken or missing shingles are a frequent occurrence, especially after Winnipeg’s notorious windstorms.
  • Moisture & Mold: Moisture damage is a serious issue, especially in older homes with inadequate ventilation.

How Routine Maintenance Can Save You Money

Regular roof maintenance is vital to keeping your roof in top condition . Without routine checks , small problems such as missing shingles can lead to significant damage .

  • Shingle Replacement: Cracked or missing shingles need to be replaced immediately .
  • Gutter Cleaning: Clearing debris from gutters can prevent water backup and leaks .
  • Roof Inspections: Scheduling a roof inspection once a year ensures small problems are caught early .

Should You Fix It Yourself or Call a Professional?

While handling roof issues on your own might seem tempting , there are potential dangers to fixing roofing issues yourself. untrained individuals can void warranties.

  • There are signs of water damage: stained ceilings are clear indications that water is seeping through the roof .
  • Your roof is aging: Roofs over 15 years old often require more frequent repairs .
  • Severe weather: Following a heavy snowstorm , it’s important to have a professional assess your roof for hidden damage.

Finding the Right Roofers for Your Home

When hiring a roofer , consider credentials . A qualified roofing company should provide licensed services . Take time to compare to ensure you're choosing the right team for your roofing project.

  • Check reviews and ratings: Look at feedback from previous clients.
  • Verify licenses and insurance: Make sure the company is bonded.
  • Ask about warranties: Ensure the company offers a warranty on workmanship .
