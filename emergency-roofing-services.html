Winnipeg’s extreme winter conditions often leads to typical roofing damage that homeowners should be aware of . These problems include leaks caused by ice dams .

  • Leaks and Ice Dams: Ice dams form when the roof’s temperature fluctuates, causing snow to melt and refreeze at the edges, leading to leaks .
  • Shingle Damage: Broken or missing shingles are a frequent occurrence, especially after Winnipeg’s notorious windstorms.
  • Moisture & Mold: Moisture damage is a serious issue, especially in older homes with inadequate ventilation.

How Routine Maintenance Can Save You Money

Regular inspections is essential to extending your roof’s lifespan. Without routine checks , small problems such as ice dams can escalate quickly .

  • Shingle Replacement: Cracked or missing shingles need to be replaced immediately .
  • Gutter Cleaning: Clearing debris from gutters can prevent water backup and leaks .
  • Roof Inspections: Scheduling a roof inspection once a year ensures small problems are caught early .

Warning Signs Your Roof Needs Professional Attention

While handling roof issues on your own might seem straightforward, there are serious drawbacks to attempting repairs without professional help . untrained individuals can cause more harm than good .

  • There are signs of water damage: peeling paint are clear indications that water is seeping through the roof .
  • Your roof is aging: Roofs over 20 years old often start showing signs of wear and tear .
  • Severe weather: After experiencing strong winds, it’s wise to have a professional assess your roof for hidden damage.

Tips for Hiring the Best Roofing Contractor

When selecting a contractor, consider customer reviews. A reputable roofing company should provide quality guarantees. Take time to compare to ensure you're choosing the right team for your roofing project.
